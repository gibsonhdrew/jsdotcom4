Specially Processed American Me

Specially Processed American Me is a performance film that explores Spam, the canned meat, as a portal into Jaime Sunwoo's Asian American upbringing and her family's experiences of the Korean War. The filmed stage production investigates Spam’s legacy in the US military and its significance during WWII in the Asia-Pacific– Korea, the Philippines, Okinawa, Hawaii, and Guam– through puppetry, masks, and original music played with kitchen instruments. Performers embody real people from Jaime's life and historical figures such as the Hormel Girls, who were women US veterans promoting Spam products through a 1950's traveling stage show, where this hybrid story takes place.
